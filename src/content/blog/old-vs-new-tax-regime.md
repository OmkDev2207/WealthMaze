## Old vs New Tax Regime: Which One Saves More Money?

With recent reforms in income tax rules, taxpayers are faced with a crucial question every year: **Should I choose the Old Tax Regime or the New Tax Regime?**

There is no single correct answer. The best regime for you depends entirely on your income level and the amount of tax deductions you can claim. Let's compare both to help you make an informed decision.

## The Old Tax Regime

The Old Tax Regime features higher tax rates but allows you to claim a wide range of exemptions and deductions to reduce your taxable income.
- **Key Deductions Allowed:** Section 80C (EPF, PPF, ELSS, Life Insurance up to ₹1.5 Lakhs), Section 80D (Health Insurance), HRA (House Rent Allowance), LTA, and Section 24(b) (Home Loan Interest up to ₹2 Lakhs).
- **Pros:** Excellent for disciplined savers who invest in tax-saving financial instruments.
- **Cons:** Complex paperwork, requires investment proof, and has higher base tax rates.

## The New Tax Regime

The New Tax Regime offers simplified, lower tax rates but requires you to forego almost all exemptions and deductions.
- **Deductions Allowed:** Standard Deduction of ₹75,000 (increased in 2024) and employer contribution to NPS (Section 80CCD(2)).
- **Pros:** Low tax rates, zero investment paperwork, and more disposable cash in hand.
- **Cons:** Does not incentivize savings, as tax benefits are not linked to investments.

## The Break-Even Point: How to Choose

To determine which regime is better, you need to calculate your **break-even deduction threshold**. This is the total deduction amount where the tax liability under both regimes is exactly the same.

As a general guideline:
- **Income under ₹7.75 Lakhs:** The New Tax Regime is usually better because of tax rebates, making your net tax liability zero.
- **Income between ₹8 Lakhs and ₹15 Lakhs:** 
  - If your total deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + Home Loan Interest) are **more than ₹2.5 Lakhs to ₹3 Lakhs**, the **Old Tax Regime** is typically better.
  - If your deductions are **less than ₹2.5 Lakhs**, the **New Tax Regime** is better.
- **Income above ₹15 Lakhs:** You need very high deductions (typically ₹3.75 Lakhs or more) to make the Old Tax Regime beneficial. For most high-earning individuals with low deductions, the New Tax Regime is more cost-effective.
