## Basics of Income Tax: An Educational Overview

Income tax is one of the most common forms of direct taxation. It is a portion of your annual earnings that is paid to the state to support public infrastructure and services. 

Understanding how income tax is structured conceptually helps demystify financial calculations.

## 1. What Counts as Income?

For tax purposes, income is not just your salary. Most jurisdictions divide income into five major heads:
- **Salary Income:** Earnings from employment, including basic pay, bonuses, and allowances.
- **House Property Income:** Rental income earned from leasing out houses or commercial buildings.
- **Business or Professional Profits:** Earnings from running a business, freelancing, or professional practices (like medicine or law).
- **Capital Gains:** Profits from selling assets like shares, mutual funds, or real estate.
- **Other Sources:** Interest from savings accounts, fixed deposits, dividends, lottery winnings, and pensions.

## 2. Taxable Income vs. Gross Income

Your **Gross Income** is the sum of all your earnings across the categories above. However, tax is calculated on your **Taxable Income** (or net income), which is gross income minus eligible deductions, exemptions, and allowances provided under local laws.

## 3. Tax Slabs and Rates

Most countries use a **progressive tax slab system**. This means your income is divided into brackets, and each bracket is taxed at a different rate:
- Income below a certain threshold is typically tax-free.
- As your income crosses into higher brackets, only the amount *within that specific bracket* is taxed at the higher rate. 
- For example, if the tax rate jumps from 5% to 10% at ₹6 Lakhs, and you earn ₹6.2 Lakhs, only the ₹20,000 above the limit is taxed at 10%, not your entire ₹6.2 Lakhs.
