## The Mathematics of Loan Prepayments

When you take out a long-term loan, you pay back a combination of principal (the borrowed amount) and interest (the cost of borrowing). Lenders calculate interest on the outstanding principal balance every month. 

Understanding the mathematical mechanics of prepayments helps explain how borrowers can reduce their interest burden over the life of a loan.

## How Loan Repayment (Amortization) works

In a standard loan, your Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) remains constant. However, the internal split between principal and interest shifts over time:
- In the early years, the outstanding principal is high, so the interest component dominates your EMI. Very little goes toward reducing the principal.
- In the later years, as the principal outstanding falls, the interest share shrinks, and principal recovery accelerates.

## The Impact of a Prepayment

A prepayment is a lump-sum payment made toward the loan principal outside of your standard EMIs. 

Because interest is calculated on the outstanding balance, making a prepayment has immediate mathematical effects:
1. **Reduces Principal Instantly:** The prepayment amount is deducted directly from the outstanding principal balance.
2. **Reduces Future Interest Charges:** Since the outstanding principal is lower, the interest calculated for all subsequent months will decrease.
3. **Creates a Choice in Restructuring:**
   - **Tenure Reduction (Recommended by math):** Keep your monthly EMI payment the same. Because the principal is lower, the loan is paid off much faster, yielding the maximum interest savings.
   - **EMI Reduction:** Keep the remaining loan tenure the same. Your outstanding balance is recalculated to produce a lower monthly EMI, providing budget relief but saving less interest overall.
