## Top Legal Tax Saving Strategies for Salaried Professionals

If you choose the Old Tax Regime, you have access to a wide range of tax-saving deductions under the Income Tax Act. By planning your investments early in the financial year, you can significantly reduce your taxable income and save lakhs in taxes.

Here are the most effective legal tax-saving strategies for salaried individuals.

## 1. Maximize Section 80C Deductions (Limit: ₹1.5 Lakhs)

Section 80C is the most popular tax-saving section. You can claim deductions up to ₹1.5 Lakhs by investing in:
- **PPF (Public Provident Fund):** A safe, government-backed 15-year savings scheme with tax-free interest.
- **ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Schemes):** Tax-saving mutual funds with a 3-year lock-in period. ELSS typically offers the highest return potential among all 80C options.
- **EPF (Employee Provident Fund):** Your monthly mandatory contribution is eligible for deduction.
- **Tuition Fees:** School and college tuition fees paid for up to two children can be claimed.

## 2. Secure Your Family with Section 80D (Health Insurance)

You can claim deductions on health insurance premiums paid for yourself, your family, and your parents:
- **Self, Spouse & Children:** Deduct up to ₹25,000 for premiums.
- **Parents (under 60):** Deduct an additional ₹25,000.
- **Parents (Senior Citizens over 60):** Deduct up to ₹50,000.
- **Preventive Health Checkup:** Claim up to ₹5,000 within the overall limit.

## 3. Claim HRA (House Rent Allowance)

If you live in rented accommodation, you can claim HRA tax exemption. To claim this, you need rent receipts and your landlord's PAN. The exempt amount is the minimum of:
1. Actual HRA received.
2. 50% of salary (for metros) or 40% (for non-metros).
3. Rent paid minus 10% of salary.

## 4. Save Additional Tax with NPS (Section 80CCD(1B))

Apart from Section 80C, you can invest up to **₹50,000 annually in the National Pension System (NPS)** and claim an exclusive deduction under Section 80CCD(1B). This is highly beneficial for individuals in the 20% or 30% tax brackets.

## 5. Leverage Home Loan Interest (Section 24(b))

If you own a home with a housing loan, you can deduct up to **₹2 Lakhs per annum** on the interest paid toward the loan under Section 24(b). If the property is let out (rented), the entire interest paid is eligible for deduction with no upper limit.
