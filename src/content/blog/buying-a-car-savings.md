## Car Savings Guide: How to Budget and Save for a New Vehicle

Buying a car is a significant financial goal. Since cars are depreciating assets that require regular maintenance, insurance, and fuel, planning your vehicle purchase systematically is essential to maintaining overall financial stability.

This guide outlines the mathematical rules for car budgeting and how to save for a car down payment or purchase price.

## The 20/4/10 Rule for Car Budgeting

Financial analysts use a standard mathematical guideline called the **20/4/10 rule** to assess vehicle affordability:
- **20% Down Payment:** Save and pay at least 20% of the vehicle's on-road price upfront. A larger down payment reduces the loan size and saves interest.
- **4-Year Finance Limit:** Cap the loan tenure at 4 years (48 months) to prevent interest costs from piling up.
- **10% Monthly Outflow:** Ensure your total monthly car expenses (EMI, insurance, fuel, maintenance) do not exceed 10% of your gross monthly income.

## How to Set Up Your Car Savings Plan

If you plan to buy a vehicle in the near future, you need a safe strategy to accumulate savings:
- **Short-Term Horizon (Under 1 Year):** Keep your car savings in highly secure and liquid instruments like bank Fixed Deposits (FD) or Recurring Deposits (RD). This protects your principal from short-term market fluctuations.
- **Medium-Term Horizon (1 to 3 Years):** You can set up an automated Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) in low-volatility debt or conservative hybrid funds. This earns higher returns than a standard bank account while preserving capital.
