## How Much Loan Can I Afford? The 35/40 Debt Rule

Banks are often eager to offer you high loan amounts based on your income. However, borrowing up to your absolute maximum limit can leave you "house poor" or struggling to cover daily expenses. 

To maintain financial health, you need to calculate your **safe borrowing limit**. Lenders and financial planners use the **35/40 Rule** to determine loan affordability.

## The 35/40 Debt Rule Explained

The 35/40 rule is a simple budget guideline that restricts your debt obligations to ensure you have enough cash left to live, save, and invest:

### 1. The 35% Principal & Interest Rule
Your monthly home loan EMI should not exceed **35% of your gross monthly income**. 
- If your gross income is ₹1,00,000 per month, your home loan EMI should ideally be capped at ₹35,000.

### 2. The 40% Total Debt Rule
Your total monthly debt obligations—including home loan EMI, car loans, credit cards, and personal credit—should not exceed **40% (up to 45% maximum) of your net take-home salary**.
- If your net monthly salary is ₹80,000, your total monthly EMI outflows should not exceed ₹32,000.

If your EMI costs exceed these limits, you are over-leveraged, leaving your budget vulnerable to unexpected income drops or emergency expenses.

## How to Determine Your Safe Loan Amount

To find out how much you can comfortably borrow:

1. **Calculate Your Available EMI Budget:** Take your net salary and multiply by 0.35. That is your maximum monthly EMI target.
2. **Reverse Calculate the Principal:** Use our EMI calculator to plug in this EMI target, current market interest rates, and your preferred tenure. Find the loan amount that produces this EMI.
3. **Factor in Maintenance Costs:** Remember that owning a home or car involves recurring costs (property tax, maintenance, fuel, insurance) that do not show up on your EMI schedule. Always keep a buffer.
4. **Build an Emergency Fund First:** Never take a loan without having 3 to 6 months of EMI payments safe in a liquid savings account. This acts as a safety net if you experience employment gaps.
