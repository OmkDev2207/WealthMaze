## Step-by-Step Income Tax Calculations with Examples

Understanding tax calculations from complex tables can be confusing. To make it clear, let's look at a step-by-step example of how income tax is computed for a salaried professional.

We will calculate the tax liability for **Suresh**, who earns a gross salary of **₹12 Lakhs per annum**, under both the Old and New Tax Regimes (using current 2024 slabs).

---

## Example Scenario: Suresh's Profile

- **Gross Salary:** ₹12,00,000
- **Rent Paid (HRA Claim):** ₹1,80,000 (qualifies for HRA exemption of ₹1,00,000)
- **Section 80C Investments (PPF + ELSS):** ₹1,50,000
- **Section 80D (Health Insurance):** ₹25,000
- **NPS Investment (Section 80CCD(1B)):** ₹50,000

---

## Option A: Old Tax Regime Calculation

Under the Old Regime, Suresh can claim all his deductions:

1. **Gross Salary:** ₹12,00,000
2. **Less Deductions:**
   - Standard Deduction: -₹50,000
   - HRA Exemption: -₹1,00,000
   - Section 80C: -₹1,50,000
   - Section 80D: -₹25,000
   - Section 80CCD(1B) (NPS): -₹50,000
3. **Net Taxable Income:**  
   \[1,200,000 - 375,000 = ₹8,25,000\]

### Tax Slabs under Old Regime:
- Up to ₹2.5 Lakhs: Nil
- ₹2.5 Lakhs to ₹5 Lakhs (5% of 2.5L): ₹12,500
- ₹5 Lakhs to ₹8.25 Lakhs (20% of 3.25L): ₹65,000
- **Total Basic Tax:** ₹12,500 + ₹65,000 = ₹77,500
- **Add 4% Health & Education Cess:** ₹3,100
- **Net Tax Payable:** **₹80,600**

---

## Option B: New Tax Regime Calculation

Under the New Regime, Suresh cannot claim HRA, 80C, 80D, or NPS. He only gets the Standard Deduction:

1. **Gross Salary:** ₹12,00,000
2. **Less Deductions:**
   - Standard Deduction: -₹75,000
3. **Net Taxable Income:**  
   \[1,200,000 - 75,000 = ₹11,25,000\]

### Tax Slabs under New Regime:
- Up to ₹3 Lakhs: Nil
- ₹3 Lakhs to ₹6 Lakhs (5% of 3L): ₹15,000
- ₹6 Lakhs to ₹9 Lakhs (10% of 3L): ₹30,000
- ₹9 Lakhs to ₹11.25 Lakhs (15% of 2.25L): ₹33,750
- **Total Basic Tax:** ₹15,000 + ₹30,000 + ₹33,750 = ₹78,750
- **Add 4% Health & Education Cess:** ₹3,150
- **Net Tax Payable:** **₹81,900**

---

## Summary of Results

For Suresh, the **Old Tax Regime saves him ₹1,300** because he makes significant investments in tax-saving instruments (totaling ₹3.75 Lakhs in exemptions). If his deductions were slightly lower (e.g. no NPS investment), the New Tax Regime would have been the cheaper option.
